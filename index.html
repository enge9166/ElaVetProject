How Mental Health can Effect Resedence

Mental health can bring multiple hardships to the lives of veterains. Addiction can lead to a downward spiral of poor finantial desisions. PTSD can keep indeviduals from making conections with others, efecting their employment. Depression can keep someone from taking action. These all are serius issues and it is important to be able to spot the signs of mental health dissorters.

Signs of Mental Health Issues

Appearing sad or depressed most of the time, with persistent feelings of emptiness or hopelessness that don't lift.

Expressing feelings that there is no reason to live, often accompanied by statements like "What's the point?" or "Things will never get better."

Experiencing unexplained guilt, shame, or sense of failure, often accompanied by feelings of worthlessness or excessive self-blame.

Exhibiting uncontrollable anger or frequent outbursts that affect relationships and daily interactions.

Engaging in dangerous activities without considering consequences, potentially endangering personal safety.

Increased use of alcohol, drugs, or prescription medications as a coping mechanism.

Diminished interest in previously enjoyable activities, including social engagements and personal hobbies.

Declining performance in work, school, or personal care, including missed deadlines and neglected duties.

Disconnecting from family and friends, leading to isolation and difficulty in seeking help.
